PUDM

  Purdue Student Sustainability Council (PSSC) is partnering with Purdue University Dance Marathon (PUDM) to make the event more environmentally friendly. The dance marathon is a major campus event involving hundreds of students dancing all night to raise money for Riley Children’s Hospital; however, the environmental impact of feeding approximately 1500 people three meals without proper kitchen facilities is huge. PSSC is working to change this! We are providing reusable plates for the major donor luncheon before the marathon to prevent the use of single use plates. For the marathon itself, PSSC is switching to compostable plates that are biodegradable.

  According to research conducted by Washington University in St. Louis, styrofoam production is extremely harmful to the environment and 25-30% of US landfills are dedicated solely to plastics, including styrofoam. In addition to compostable dining ware, we are diverting all food waste to the West Lafayette biodigestor, which converts food waste into electric power instead of letting it rot in the landfill. Currently, food waste creates approximately 20% of the power needed for the municipal wastewater treatment plant, and there is room to grow!

Last Plastic Straw

  "The committee strives to raise awareness about the issue of plastic in the environment and ways of decreasing it's use. They are campaigning to create a base of students to support this issue and support a resolution led by the West Lafayette City Council in reducing plastic straw use in the city. The committee believes in reducing nonbiodegradable waste, while keeping in mind that those dependent on them, may not have practical or economical alternatives."

Friday Night Lights

  Friday Night Lights (FNL) is a project run by a subcommittee of the Student Sustainability Council, whose mission is to collect data on building energy usage and savings at Purdue University to encourage/inspire energy efficient changes. Every Friday, a new group of volunteers visits thirteen different buildings on the campus to turn off lights for the weekend. Each volunteer group receives a data sheet to collect the information on how many lights are being turned off in the specified rooms. This data is then compiled over time and used to produce figures on electricity usage/costs that help back the argument for furthering industrial sustainability on campus. Since its start in 2013, FNL has saved Purdue up to $13, 212.48 in energy costs.
